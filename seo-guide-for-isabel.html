Getting Found Online

Three things you can do to make sure people searching for a therapist in the South Bay actually find you. Each one builds on the last.

What's already done

Your website is already set up with technical SEO — structured data that tells Google you're a therapist in the South Bay, optimized images, keyword-rich page titles, and three new landing pages targeting specific searches like "anxiety therapy south bay" and "telehealth counseling california."

What's below is the off-site work — things that happen outside your website that tell Google (and potential clients) you're real, credible, and worth showing in search results. This is where you come in.

1

Claim Your Google Business Profile

Highest Impact
Why this matters: When someone Googles "therapist near me" or "counseling south bay," Google shows a map with local businesses first — before any website. Without a Google Business Profile, you're invisible in that map. This is the single most important thing you can do for local SEO.

Here's how to set it up:

  1. Go to Google Business Profile: Visit business.google.com and sign in with your Google account (your isabelsimoncounseling@gmail.com works perfectly).
  2. Search for your business: Type "Isabel Simon Counseling" — if it already exists as an unclaimed listing, claim it. If not, click "Add your business."
  3. Choose your category: Select "Counselor" as the primary category. You can also add "Mental Health Service" and "Psychotherapist" as additional categories.
  4. Set your service area: Since you likely don't want your home address published, choose "I deliver goods and services to my customers" and list your service areas: Redondo Beach, Hermosa Beach, Manhattan Beach, Torrance, Palos Verdes, El Segundo, San Pedro.
  5. Add your details:
    • Phone: (323) 391-6783
    • Website: www.isabelsimoncounseling.com
    • Hours: Monday – Friday, 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM (or your actual hours)
  6. Verify your business: Google will ask you to verify — usually by postcard, phone, or email. This can take a few days. Complete the verification to make your profile live.
  7. Add a description: Something like: "Isabel Simon is an Associate Professional Clinical Counselor (APCC) in the South Bay specializing in anxiety, depression, life transitions, and adolescent/young adult therapy. In-person and virtual sessions available."
  8. Add photos: Upload your headshot (the one on your website) and your logo. Profiles with photos get 42% more requests for directions and 35% more clicks.
Ongoing: Once it's set up, try to post an update every few weeks — even something short like "Now accepting new clients for evening sessions" or sharing a mental health awareness tip. Google rewards active profiles.
2

Submit Your Site to Google Search Console

High Impact
Why this matters: Google Search Console tells Google your website exists and gives it a map of all your pages (your sitemap). Without it, Google might take weeks or months to discover your new pages on its own. With it, your pages can show up in search within days.
  1. Go to Search Console: Visit search.google.com/search-console and sign in with your Google account.
  2. Add your property: Click "Add Property" and choose "URL prefix". Enter: https://www.isabelsimoncounseling.com
  3. Verify ownership: The easiest method is "HTML tag" — Google will give you a meta tag to add to your site. Send it to Jake and he'll add it in 2 minutes. Alternatively, if you have access to your domain's DNS settings, you can verify that way too.
  4. Submit your sitemap: Once verified, go to Sitemaps in the left sidebar and enter: sitemap.xml then click Submit.
What you'll get: After a week or two, Search Console will start showing you which Google searches lead people to your site, how often you appear, and where you rank. This is gold for understanding what's working.
3

Create Therapist Directory Profiles

High Impact
Why this matters: Therapist directories rank extremely high on Google — often above individual therapist websites. A Psychology Today profile alone can be a major source of new clients. These profiles also create backlinks to your website, which tells Google your site is trustworthy and boosts your ranking.

Create profiles on these directories, in order of priority:

  1. Psychology Today — psychologytoday.com/us/therapists/signup
    This is the #1 therapist directory. It costs ~$30/month but is often the top organic result for "therapist in [city]." Fill out every field — specialties, approach, insurance, personal statement. Use a warm, first-person writing style (similar to your website). Include your website URL.
  2. TherapyDen — therapyden.com/for-therapists
    Free to list. More progressive/inclusive positioning. Good for reaching younger clients. Include your specialties and a link to your website.
  3. GoodTherapy — goodtherapy.org/for-professionals.html
    Another well-known directory with strong Google rankings. Paid listing (~$30/month) but well-trafficked.

For each profile, make sure to include:

  • Your website URL: www.isabelsimoncounseling.com
  • Your phone number and email (consistent across all profiles)
  • All your specialties: anxiety, depression, life transitions, adolescents/young adults, eating disorders, self-esteem, stress management
  • Your modalities: CBT, mindfulness, narrative therapy, trauma-informed care, strengths-based
  • Service formats: in-person (South Bay) and telehealth (California-wide)
  • Your license: APCC #15186
Consistency is key: Make sure your name, phone number, and address are exactly the same across every profile and your website. Google cross-references these — inconsistencies hurt your ranking.
