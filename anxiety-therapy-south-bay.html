Understanding Anxiety

Anxiety is one of the most common mental health challenges people face — and it is nothing to be ashamed of. Millions of adults experience anxiety each year, and it can show up in ways you might not expect.

You might notice a racing heart before a meeting, a knot in your stomach that never quite goes away, or a constant loop of "what if" thoughts that keep you up at night. Maybe you feel on edge without knowing why, or find yourself avoiding situations that used to feel easy.

Anxiety affects both the body and the mind. Physical symptoms like muscle tension, headaches, fatigue, and trouble sleeping often go hand-in-hand with emotional signs like irritability, difficulty concentrating, and a sense of dread. These experiences are real, and they are your body's way of telling you something needs attention.

The good news is that anxiety is highly treatable. Whether you are in Redondo Beach, Hermosa Beach, Manhattan Beach, or Torrance, the right support can help you understand your anxiety, manage your symptoms, and reconnect with the life you want to live.

How Therapy Helps with Anxiety

Therapy gives you a space to understand what is driving your anxiety — not just the surface-level symptoms, but the deeper patterns, beliefs, and experiences that keep you stuck in a cycle of worry.

In our work together, I draw from several evidence-based approaches tailored to your unique needs:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) helps you identify and shift the thought patterns that fuel anxiety. You will learn practical tools to challenge unhelpful thinking and respond to stressful situations with more clarity and confidence.
  • Mindfulness-based techniques teach you how to stay grounded in the present moment instead of getting swept away by worry about the future. These skills help calm your nervous system and build emotional resilience over time.
  • Somatic and body-based approaches address the physical side of anxiety. By tuning into what your body is telling you, you can release stored tension and develop a deeper sense of safety and ease.

Whether you are dealing with generalized anxiety, social anxiety, panic attacks, or stress that has become unmanageable, therapy can help you build a toolkit of strategies that actually work in your daily life.

What to Expect in Anxiety Counseling

Starting therapy can feel like a big step — especially when anxiety is the very thing you are seeking help for. I want you to know that your first session is simply a conversation. There is no pressure, no judgment, and no expectation to have everything figured out.

In our initial session, we will talk about what brought you to therapy, what you are experiencing day to day, and what you are hoping to change. This helps me understand your unique situation so we can create a personalized plan that fits your life and your goals.

Every person's experience with anxiety is different, which is why I do not take a one-size-fits-all approach. Your therapy will be shaped around you — your pace, your comfort level, and what feels most meaningful for your growth.

I offer both in-person sessions in Redondo Beach and telehealth appointments throughout California, so you can choose the format that works best for you. Many clients in Hermosa Beach, Manhattan Beach, Torrance, and Palos Verdes find virtual therapy to be a convenient and effective option.

My goal is to create a warm, safe space where you feel truly heard. You do not have to navigate anxiety alone — and reaching out for support is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Signs You May Benefit from Anxiety Therapy

You might benefit from working with an anxiety therapist if you are experiencing any of the following:

  • Persistent worry or racing thoughts that are hard to control
  • Feeling restless, on edge, or unable to relax
  • Avoiding social situations, work tasks, or everyday activities because of fear or dread
  • Difficulty sleeping, or waking up feeling anxious
  • Physical symptoms like a tight chest, stomach problems, headaches, or muscle tension
  • Irritability or mood swings that feel out of proportion
  • Difficulty concentrating or feeling like your mind goes blank
  • Panic attacks or sudden waves of intense fear
  • Relying on avoidance, perfectionism, or overworking to cope
  • Feeling like anxiety is holding you back from the life you want

If any of these resonate with you, know that these are common experiences — and they do not have to define your life. Anxiety therapy in the South Bay can help you move from surviving to thriving.
