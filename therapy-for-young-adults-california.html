Based in the South Bay and specializing in adolescents and young adults, Isabel offers both in-person and virtual therapy sessions throughout California.

Navigating the Unique Challenges of Young Adulthood

Being a young adult today comes with its own set of challenges that previous generations didn't face. Whether you're heading off to college, starting your first job, or trying to figure out who you are and what you want, it can feel like the pressure is coming from every direction.

From managing the transition to college life and figuring out career goals, to navigating complex relationships and the constant pull of social media, young adulthood is a time of major change. It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed, anxious, or uncertain during this stage of life. And it's also the perfect time to build the tools and self-awareness that will serve you for years to come.

Therapy gives you a safe, private space to work through these experiences with someone who truly understands what you're going through. You don't need to have everything figured out to get started.

Isabel's Approach to Young Adult Therapy

Isabel gets it. She's relatable, warm, and genuinely non-judgmental. Her goal is to create a space where you feel comfortable being yourself, whether that means venting about a tough week, unpacking something deeper, or simply figuring out your next step.

Her therapeutic approach draws on Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), narrative therapy, and strengths-based techniques. In practice, that means she helps you identify unhelpful thought patterns, rewrite the stories that hold you back, and build on the strengths you already have.

With a background in art history, Isabel brings a creative and perceptive lens to her work. She's skilled at seeing patterns and perspectives that others might miss, and she uses that insight to help you connect the dots in your own life. Sessions with Isabel don't feel clinical or rigid. They feel like a real conversation with someone who's in your corner.

Common Concerns

Young adults and adolescents in the South Bay and across California come to therapy for many different reasons. You might relate to one of these, or you might be dealing with something else entirely. Either way, Isabel is here to help.

  • Anxiety — racing thoughts, worry, feeling on edge or overwhelmed
  • Depression — low mood, loss of motivation, feeling disconnected
  • Self-esteem — negative self-talk, comparison, not feeling "good enough"
  • Eating disorders — struggles with body image, food, and control
  • Life transitions — college, moving, new jobs, changes in family dynamics
  • Academic stress — pressure to perform, burnout, perfectionism
  • Relationship issues — friendships, dating, family conflict, boundaries
  • Identity exploration — understanding who you are and what matters to you

If you're not sure whether therapy is right for you, that's okay. A free consultation is a great way to find out.

In-Person & Virtual Sessions

Life as a young adult is busy. Between classes, work, and everything else, finding time for therapy can feel like a challenge. That's why Isabel offers flexible scheduling with both in-person and telehealth options.

In-person sessions are available in the South Bay area, including Redondo Beach and the greater Los Angeles area. If you prefer meeting face-to-face, the office provides a calm, welcoming environment where you can feel at ease.

Virtual sessions are available to anyone located in California. Telehealth makes it easy to attend therapy from your dorm room, your apartment, or wherever you feel most comfortable. All you need is a private space and a reliable internet connection.

Whether you're a college student in the Los Angeles area or a young adult anywhere in California, Isabel can work with you to find a format and schedule that fits your life.

Ready to take the first step? Schedule a free 15-minute consultation to see if working together feels like the right fit. There's no pressure and no commitment — just an honest conversation about what you're going through and how therapy might help.
